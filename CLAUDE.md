# Project context — AI vendor governance platform

## What this product is

A B2B SaaS platform that helps IT-focused companies (SaaS, fintech, healthtech, agencies) generate AI usage policies and internal governance documentation to control how their vendors use AI on their projects. The primary deliverable is the policy, the documentation, and the audit-ready paper trail. AI training is a complementary module that gives that documentation credibility — it is not the product.

**The product hierarchy:**
1. **Primary:** Policy generation, governance documentation, completion records — the paper trail companies need to prove vendor AI compliance
2. **Complementary:** AI training module — what vendors complete to make the documentation meaningful

The platform is not a training business. Training is the mechanism that makes the policy acknowledgement credible. The paper trail is what companies are actually buying.

**One-sentence user value (company buyer):** "Your vendors are using AI on your projects — you have no idea how."

**One-sentence business value:** "Sell the paper trail that protects companies from their vendors' AI mistakes."

---

## What this product is NOT

- Not a competitor to DataCamp, Coursera, or EXIN — those are training platforms for individuals
- Not a general AI compliance tool (not competing with Vanta, Drata, OneTrust)
- Not a certification authority issuing regulated professional credentials
- Not a consumer product — fully B2B across all tiers
- **Not an AI training platform** — training is a complementary module, not the product. Never position it as a training or certification product. The product is vendor AI governance and policy documentation.

## Legal and liability positioning (critical — never deviate)

The platform is an **attestation and record-keeping platform**, not a certifying authority. This distinction must be preserved in all copy, documents, and product decisions.

- Completion records confirm that training was delivered and acknowledged on a specific date
- They do NOT verify actual vendor conduct, guarantee regulatory compliance, or assert that a vendor "uses AI responsibly"
- Layer 2 records are issued by the **client company**, not the platform
- The platform is the delivery and signature mechanism — analogous to DocuSign, not a notary
- Every completion record must include: "Completion of this acknowledgement confirms only that the above policy was received and read on the date shown. It does not verify past or future conduct."
- Terms of Service must include a liability cap (fees paid in last 12 months), B2B-only declaration, and the attestation disclaimer

## Two-layer product model

**Primary layer — Policy and governance documentation**

This is what companies are buying. The policy wizard generates a company-specific AI usage policy. Vendors read it, acknowledge it, and a timestamped completion record is produced. That record is the deliverable — auditable, defensible, and on record.

**Complementary layer — AI training module**

Training is what makes the policy acknowledgement credible. A vendor who has completed structured AI training before acknowledging a policy carries more weight than one who simply clicked through. The training module gives the documentation its credibility — but it is never sold as the primary product.

**Layer 1 — Baseline training certificate** *(complementary)*
- Issued by: the platform
- Content: AI fundamentals, responsible use, data hygiene, prompt safety
- Earnable independently before working with any specific company
- Produces: shareable PDF cert + verifiable badge link
- Valid 12 months, renewable
- **Role:** Gives Layer 2 credibility. Not the headline product.

**Layer 2 — Company policy acknowledgement** *(primary)*
- Issued by: the client company (platform facilitates, company signs off)
- Content: company-specific AI policy module + e-signature acknowledgement
- Requires Layer 1 as prerequisite
- Produces: timestamped completion record (not marketed as a "certificate")
- Called "completion record" or "policy acknowledgement" — never "certificate"
- **Role:** This is what companies pay for. The paper trail. The documentation.

**Positioning rule — never invert this hierarchy:**
- Do not lead with "AI certification" in any marketing copy
- Do not describe the platform as a "training platform" or "certification platform"
- Always lead with policy generation, governance documentation, vendor control
- Training is mentioned as the mechanism that makes documentation meaningful, not as the product itself

## B2B-only structure (important)

All four tiers are B2B. The Founder plan converts freelancers to business buyers:
- Business name field at signup (free text, no verification required — "Jane Smith Consulting" is valid)
- Checkout confirmation checkbox: "I confirm I am purchasing on behalf of a business or in my professional capacity"
- The checkbox + ToS B2B declaration does the legal work — the form field alone does not

**Critical nuance established in the session:** If a user enters a random string or their personal first name into the business name field, the transaction is still economically B2C regardless of what the form says. EU consumer protection law looks at substance, not what a field label says. **The form field is UX only — it is not legal protection.** What actually protects you is:
1. The active confirmation checkbox (required, not pre-ticked)
2. The ToS clause stating "by purchasing you confirm you are acting in a professional capacity"
3. The product being inherently professional in nature — nobody buys vendor AI governance documentation as a consumer hobby

The business name field still belongs in the form to reinforce professional framing and create the right mental context for the buyer. But treat it as UX, not legal defence.

- US B2B customers: no VAT — US has no VAT system
- EU B2B customers: reverse charge mechanism — collect their VAT ID, validate via VIES, write "reverse charge" on invoice
- EU non-VAT-registered buyers (some Founder plan): Paddle/Lemon Squeezy handles this automatically
- Slovak VAT registration threshold: €49,790 domestic turnover — not relevant at launch

**Accountant:** Required now, before revenue starts. Budget €600–€1,200/yr.

**Bank account:** The s.r.o. almost certainly already has its own business bank account separate from personal accounts — this is standard for any registered s.r.o. No action needed unless revenue has been going into a personal account, in which case open a dedicated business account now before transactions start. Reason is practical not legal: clean books from day one means a cheaper accountant bill and cleaner tax records. Paddle/Lemon Squeezy or Stripe payouts should go to the s.r.o. account, not a personal account.

**Cyber insurance:** Before first Scale plan customer. €40–€80/mo via Colonnade or Kooperatíva.

## Viral loop mechanic (core acquisition engine — do not lose this)

Every vendor who completes the cert and puts it on their Upwork or LinkedIn profile is a walking advertisement. The loop works as follows:

1. A company (ICP #1 or #3) signs up and invites their vendor team
2. Vendor completes Layer 1 cert, puts badge on their professional profile
3. Other companies see the badge → ask what it is → find the platform
4. Those companies become new Company plan customers → invite their vendors
5. Repeat

This only works if the certificate and completion record look genuinely credible — clean design, specific language, verifiable badge link. Design investment in these documents is a direct acquisition cost.

The Founder plan feeds this loop from the vendor side: freelancers who earn the cert proactively carry the brand into every new client relationship before they're even invited through a company's Layer 2 flow.

## EU AI Act — regulatory tailwind (core sales context)

This is the single most important external factor supporting the business. Reference it in sales conversations.

**Key facts established in the planning session:**
- From February 2025, the EU AI Act requires providers and deployers of AI systems to ensure employees and contractors using AI have adequate AI literacy — including training
- This obligation covers **vendors and contractors**, not just employees — the exact target market for this platform
- Fines for non-compliance: up to €35M or 7% of global annual turnover
- The law applies extraterritorially: US companies with EU employees or EU-facing products are also covered
- Many EU companies use certification as a way to evidence Article 4 compliance — your platform's completion records serve this purpose

**The sales pitch implication:** Companies aren't just buying peace of mind — they're buying documented evidence they can point to if a regulator or enterprise client asks "how do you ensure your AI vendors comply with your standards?"

## Agency ICP decision — important nuance

**Mature agencies (10+ staff, established client roster) are NOT a good primary ICP.** They will say "we'll do it ourselves," "we already train our staff," or "our NDA covers this." They have thought about it, have something in place, and don't feel acute pain. Trying to sell them cold wastes time.

**Small and growing agencies (2–8 people, freelancer-heavy) are the right sub-segment** within ICP #3. They:
- Don't have internal training infrastructure
- Rely heavily on subcontractors they barely manage
- Are actively trying to win larger clients who are asking about AI governance
- Want the credential without having to build it

The agency ICP is secondary for another reason: they often think of themselves as the company, not the vendor — so there is identity confusion about who your product is for. ICP #1 (product companies) has no such confusion.

- Onboarding calls for Company plan customers (async Loom only)
- Content updates outside the quarterly review cycle
- Custom reporting or dashboard features
- Anything that requires your personal time per customer below Scale plan level

**The rule:** If a Company plan customer asks for something not in the standard product, the answer is "that's on our roadmap" or "that's available on the Scale plan." Never agree to build it ad-hoc.

**Why this matters:** The self-serve model only works if it is actually self-serve. The moment you start hand-holding Company plan customers, your time is consumed and the business model breaks.

## AI behaviour rules — for all Claude and Claude Code sessions

These rules apply to every session that reads this file. They are not suggestions. They override default helpful behaviour when in conflict with accuracy.

---

### Rule 1 — Never assume, always verify

Before making any claim about the following categories, stop and search for current information:

- Pricing of any tool, platform, or service mentioned in this project
- Current state of EU AI Act enforcement, timelines, or requirements
- Current state of Slovak tax law, VAT thresholds, or accounting requirements
- Competitor products or their features (Vanta, Drata, DataCamp, EXIN, TalentLMS, iSpring, Paddle, Lemon Squeezy, Cookieyes, Iubenda, Arcade, Navattic, etc.)
- Market size figures, growth rates, or adoption statistics
- Any legal requirement in any jurisdiction
- Platform capabilities (Skool, Circle, Mighty Networks, or any LMS)
- Conversion rate benchmarks or SaaS metrics

If current information cannot be retrieved, say so explicitly. Do not fill the gap with training data presented as current fact.

---

### Rule 2 — Distinguish fact from inference

Every response must be internally honest about the source of its claims:

- **Verified (searched this session):** state the source
- **From project context (this CLAUDE.md):** reference it as such
- **From training data (not verified):** flag it explicitly — "this is from my training data and may be outdated"
- **Inferred / reasoned:** label it as inference, not fact

Never present an inference as a researched finding. Never present a training-data recollection as a current fact.

---

### Rule 3 — No hallucination of specifics

The following types of content must never be generated without a verifiable source:

- Specific pricing figures for third-party tools (e.g. "TalentLMS costs €X/month")
- Specific legal penalty amounts or enforcement thresholds
- Named statistics with percentages or figures (e.g. "74% of companies...")
- Named competitor features or product capabilities
- Named regulatory deadlines
- Quotes attributed to specific people or organisations

If a specific figure is needed and cannot be verified in this session, say "I don't have a verified current figure for this — search [specific source] to confirm."

---

### Rule 4 — Challenge before executing

If a request would result in reversing a decision already documented in this file, do not execute silently. Instead:

1. Flag that the request conflicts with a documented decision
2. State what the documented decision is and why it was made
3. Ask whether the intention is to override the decision or whether this is a misunderstanding

Documented decisions that must not be silently reversed:
- Pricing structure (Founder / Company / Scale — Starter and Professional are removed)
- No free trial (replaced by 14-day money-back guarantee)
- B2B-only model (including Founder plan framing)
- Two-layer cert model and language conventions (Layer 2 is not a certificate)
- Skool is not suitable — decision made, do not re-recommend
- Slovak s.r.o. entity — no need for US incorporation
